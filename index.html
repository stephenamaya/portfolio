About Me:

I am a Designer and Developer living in Orlando, FL. I studied and earned my degree in web development at the University of Central Florida. With a graphic design background, I enjoy using both perspectives to create easy breezy user experiences and interfaces.

Currently working as an in-house web developer, I focus on creating web solutions for a wide variety of business applications. Apart from designing and developing, I am full time tree hugger a as well as a part time jet engine mechanic in the Air Force Reserves - classic combo.

Nitty Gritty:

I spend most of my time creating with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as dabbling in common frameworks and tools. When not in code - mode, I primarially design and animate in Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign, and XD.

Lesters Garden Bistro

Lesters Garden Bistro is a project for a silly goose named Lester. "Lesters Garden Bistro" is the name for his business that offers many event space services, while being operated by animals. Lester has quickly become a local celebrity, and even has his own merchandise. I decided to create this website to honor the strange goose and family that keeps the bistro alive.

Minty

Minty is a combination of an HTML boilerplate, CSS styling, and pre-organized code. After creating an abundance of static websites, I decided to make this downloadable template utilizing the fastest and simplest methods I have gathered. Minty combines my big 3; cross browser ease, simple noted code, and minimal external resources. All of this creates a great starting point with a 100% Google Lighthouse rating.

Weather Finder

Weather Finder is a web application showing you the weather based on the country and city. It was created in React, utilizing the Open Weather Map API to gather its data. The project was an explorartion of simple UI and UX, utilizing a single color palette and ample whitespace.

Superman

The "Superman" project was a exploration of poster design, and fan art for the 1999 movie, The Iron Giant. Following the bold color palette of retro movie posters in the spirit of fall in Rockwell, Maine. The design combines the iconic foreshadowing, and tragic end of the film.

SEED

Sustainable Electric Envorinment Dispenser, or SEED, is a poster design for an event at the University of Central Florida. The prompt was to design futuristic solutions to sustainability related issues. Through sketching I conceptualized SEED, an electrically powered robot that could autonomously plant seeds for future habitats and food.
